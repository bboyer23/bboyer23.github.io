My Story

My path hasn't been linear, and it hasn't always been clear. But every challenge, every restart, and every mistake taught me something valuable. This is the story of how I learned to keep building — one step at a time.

Where It Started

I grew up learning resilience out of necessity. Losing my mom young taught me that life doesn't wait, and that I needed to figure things out for myself. That mindset — keep learning, keep moving forward — became the foundation for everything after.

Music, tech, and curiosity were my anchors. I wasn't coding yet, but I was developing a mindset that would shape everything after: stay curious, learn from what interests you, and be willing to adapt.

The Dip — And the Reset

COVID hit harder than I expected. I struggled through 2020–2021, lost momentum, and eventually stepped back from school to regroup. It felt like failure at the time. In hindsight, it was necessary.

During that time, I worked to stabilize myself — financially and mentally. No grand plans, just daily progress. Every small step forward mattered. That's when I learned that growth isn't always visible, and that's okay.

Building My Foundation

When I returned to college at Framingham State University, I came back with purpose. I wasn’t just there to pass classes — I was there to build a future I could trust.

I dove into:

  • Computer Science I & II (Java, OOP, fundamentals)
  • Operating Systems & UNIX
  • Networking Technologies
  • Database Management
  • Cloud Computing & AI Concepts
  • Quantitative & Statistical Analysis

These weren’t just courses. They were bricks — solid, reliable — in the foundation I was rebuilding for myself.

Becoming a Builder

As my confidence grew, so did my ambition. I started building real projects — each one bigger and more complex than the last.

ChronoBox

A local cloud device built from the ground up — Debian 13, RAID, Nextcloud, hardware design, and security.

Smart Return Tracker

A Dockerized Express + PostgreSQL full-stack project simulating enterprise retail workflows.

FEDC Capstone Lead

Led a 6-person team delivering a full Squarespace website to a real economic development organization.

These projects weren’t homework anymore — they were proof that I could take an idea, build it, and deliver it.

Learning in Professional Settings

I've been fortunate to work in roles that challenged me in different ways:

  • IT Analyst Intern @ TJX — Learning Agile workflows, ServiceNow, and how large teams coordinate
  • IBM Accelerate — Exploring AI solutions and understanding how companies approach client problems
  • Cloud Solved MSP — Building hands-on experience with Azure, security practices, and hybrid systems
  • Years at Shaw's — Developing leadership skills by supporting people and managing operations

Each role showed me a different perspective — how teams work, how systems fail, how communication matters.

Where I’m Heading

My journey taught me three things:

  • You don’t need a perfect start, only a direction.
  • You grow by breaking things, fixing them, and not being afraid of the process.
  • Forward is the only direction that matters.

I’m on track to graduate in December 2025 — and I’m building myself into someone capable of creating systems, leading teams, and tackling any challenge, no matter how complex.

This is where I’ve been. But more importantly — it’s the foundation for everything I’m building next.
