Featured Publication

A New Era of Cloud Computing: The Local Centralized Private Cloud Model

The Premise

As public cloud breaches become statistically inevitable, the "Trust but Verify" model is failing. This paper proposes a hybrid architectural model: The Local Centralized Private Cloud. It argues for a shift back to physical data ownership for sensitive use-cases, leveraging modern containerization to maintain usability without sacrificing sovereignty.

Methodology

  • Quantitative Analysis: Statistical review of major cloud breaches (2020-2024), analyzing vector and scale.
  • Qualitative Survey: Assessed consumer sentiment regarding AI training on user data vs. privacy expectations.
  • Architectural Proposal: Technical specifications for a Debian/Nextcloud implementation (ChronoBox).

Areas of Inquiry

My current interests are no longer oriented in any one direction, but rather at the crossroads of several emerging trends: cloud trust erosion, data sovereignty, AI ethics, and decentralized infrastructure. I am particularly focused on how individuals and organizations can reclaim agency over their digital assets in an era of pervasive surveillance and commodification of data.
